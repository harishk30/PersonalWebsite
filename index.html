About Me

Hello! I'm Harish, a sophomore at Princeton University majoring in Computer Science and Physics.

Lately, I've been working on computer vision research with Dr. Zhuang Liu from Meta FAIR. Additionally, I've been working on computational physics research, using machine learning and statistics for various physical applications, from understanding the universe to taming fusion reactions.

This summer, I'll be working with LLMs as a part of the Amazon AGI (Artificial General Intellegence) team.

Outside of work, you can find me taking pictures under the stars. I'm an award-winning astrophotographer (you can also check out my photos in the gallery above), and have been featured by NASA, the SETI Institute, and major telescope companies. I also am an avid singer, and particularly enjoy R&B.

Research

The research I've worked on includes:

  • Computer Vision research with Dr. Zhuang Liu.
  • Applying Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to astrophysical problems. I'm working on this with Dr. Francisco Villaescusa-Navarro at the Center for Computational Astrophysics. (Github)
  • Studying dwarf satellite galaxies in xSAGA data, and analyzing how host environment affects their formation. I'm working on this with Dr. John Wu from the Space Telescope Science Institute. (Github)
  • Applied transformers for plasma control at the Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory (PPPL).
  • Discovering 1151 previously unclassified ring galaxies with a machine model I created, working with Dr. Bryce Kalmbach from the DiRAC Institute. This work was published in the Astronomical Journal, won awards at Regeneron ISEF & STS, and was presented at the AAS conference. (Github | Research Paper)
